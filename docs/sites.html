Site Name: Fort Halifax

Site Code: 36DA0008

Fort Halifax is a French and Indian War fort built in 1756 by Colonel William Clapham as part of the Augusta Expedition. Located in Halifax, Pennsylvania, it was situated along the Susquehanna to patrol the river's use by French-allied Native American tribes. Three forts were built during the Augusta Expedition: Fort Hunter, Fort Halifax, and Fort Augusta. Once Fort Augusta was completed, Halifax was eventually abandoned and demolished, making it a unique case study for archaeologists.
