OUR STORY

The spark for this inititative was ignited during the 2022 Spring Semester during a consultation between Dr. Burns and the student-led IDT. The vision was to develop K-12 curriculum focus on local sites while providing students with an archaeology experience in a plastic showcase box; Archaeology in a Box (AiB).

The project was placed on hold as the CRI and IDT focused their efforts on improving the CRI Lab workflow processes. During the AiB project hiatus, project scope/planning went through several iterations and the effort was rekindled as the IDT relocated to the newly renovated Statton Learning Commons, 2024 Spring semester.

The AiB project gained focus during the 2024 Summer/Fall Semester as key assets and human resources where reestablished in the Statton Learning Commons.
